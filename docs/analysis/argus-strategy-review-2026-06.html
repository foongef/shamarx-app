Designation

ARGUS

Adaptive Reversal Gating at Untapped Swing-liquidity. An H1 stop-hunt engine that waits for the market to raid resting liquidity, then fades or rides the move — depending on what the daily trend says.

Named for the hundred-eyed watchman of Greek myth — Argus Panoptes never slept, watching every swing extreme for the wick that gives the raid away. Previously known internally as stop-hunt reversal / SMC sweep.

Is it profitable?
VALIDATED
+179% over 28 months in replay, 790 trades, 65.8% WR. The edge is real in backtest and survived three adversarial gate studies.
Is it HIGHLY profitable?
NOT YET PROVEN
The first live week produced only 23 genuine trades (35% WR, +$40.62) — the rest was infrastructure noise, now fixed. Clean validation starts now; judge after 60–90 real trades.
Is it safe?
YES · BY DESIGN
Five-layer risk stack: daily-loss breaker, loss-streak pauses, tiered drawdown brakes, adaptive sizing, and a 40% hard-kill. Among the best retail risk frameworks reviewed.

◆ Section · 02 Mechanics

How ARGUS hunts

01
Watch the swing extremes
3-bar fractals on H1 mark where retail stops cluster. Lookback is pair-tuned: 24–32 bars.
02
Detect the raid
A wick pierces the extreme by sweepBuffer × ATR but the body closes back inside — institutional stop-hunt signature. The failed breakout IS the signal.
03
Ask the daily chart which way to trade it
D1 ADX ≥ 22 → CONTINUATION (ride the trend through the sweep). Below → REVERSAL (fade the raid). A structure-health filter vetoes CONTINUATION when ADX is falling, EMAs misaligned, or trend exhausted (ADX > 50).
04
Strike only in killzones
London + NY sessions (plus Tokyo for USDJPY). Signals stay valid in a pending queue for 8–12 H1 bars; any M15 close inside the window can trigger.
05
Manage the position mechanically
30% off at TP1 (0.8R) → break-even stop at +1R → trail from +2R toward TP2 (3.5–4.0R). No discretion anywhere in the loop.

◆ Section · 04 What could hurt you

Honest weaknesses

№1 — The live health panel was showing poisoned numbers Fixed · 2026-06-11
Root cause found in prod: 60 of 83 closed trades were ORPHAN reconciliation artifacts at pnl=0, finalized in one bad pass on June 8 when a freshly-reconnected MetaApi client returned an empty position list. They dragged measured WR to ~12% against the 65% baseline. Fixed three ways: analytics exclude ORPHAN rows; the position-monitor requires 3 consecutive absent reads before finalizing a close; and orchestrator dedup state is now restored before first use, ending the duplicate entries (one sweep had been traded 8×).
№2 — Backtest-to-live gap is unproven
+179% came from replayed candles with modeled fills. Live adds spread variance, slippage on news wicks, MetaApi/cTrader latency, and partial-fill behavior the simulator can't see. A realistic haircut is 30–50% of backtest returns. Still profitable — but plan on +90–125% per 28 months, not +179%, until live data says otherwise.
№3 — Regime dependence with no regime detector
The 2024–2026 window contained strong trends (gold's run, USDJPY intervention cycles). ARGUS profits from raids on liquidity in directional markets. A prolonged ultra-low-volatility regime (2019-style) would starve it: fewer sweeps, more chop-fakeouts. The D1 ADX floor partially protects, but nothing measures "is this market still the market we validated on?"
№4 — EURUSD / GBPUSD correlation is unmodeled
Both pairs share killzones and direction most days (~0.85 correlated). Simultaneous signals are effectively one 3% bet wearing two 1.5% hats. The risk manager counts positions, not exposure.
№5 — Dormancy is a feature that feels like a bug
Killzones + ADX floor + dedup means the engine does nothing for large stretches. That discipline IS the edge — but it also means slow equity growth in quiet months and temptation to "loosen the filters" (every loosening tested so far made it worse).
№6 — Single point of failure history
The MetaApi outage proved bridge risk is real. Spec 3 (cTrader OAuth, just shipped) addresses this — once the Spotware app is Active and accounts run on both bridges, this weakness retires.
◆ Section · 05 From profitable to highly profitable

Upgrade path

Tier 1 · Now
Trust your instruments
Fix the expectancy-drift calculation
Guard against riskAmount ≤ ε rows, exclude replay/manual trades from the live sample, add a unit test with a poisoned row. The DEGRADED panel must be believable before anything else matters.
~half day
Live-vs-backtest shadow ledger
For every live signal, log what the simulator would have done at the same candle. The divergence series (fill slippage, missed entries, spread cost) is the single most valuable dataset you can start collecting today — every later improvement is calibrated against it.
~2 days
Finish the news blackout
newsBlackoutMinutes is configured but not fully wired. NFP/FOMC/CPI wicks are exactly the fake-sweep generator ARGUS is most vulnerable to. Complete the calendar feed integration.
~2 days
Tier 2 · Next
Structural intelligence
Correlation-aware exposure cap
Treat same-direction EURUSD+GBPUSD as one exposure bucket: when both signal within the same killzone, take the higher-conviction one or halve both sizes. Directly addresses weakness №4 with zero new ML.
~2 days
Regime monitor + auto-throttle
A rolling 60-day fingerprint (realized vol, ADX distribution, sweep frequency) vs the validation window. When the live regime drifts beyond 2σ from anything in the backtest, cut risk to 0.75× automatically and flag the dashboard. The strategy doesn't change — its confidence does.
~4 days
Quarterly walk-forward revalidation cron
Auto-rerun the 28-month replay quarterly with the window slid forward; alert if WR or expectancy degrade beyond thresholds. Catches slow parameter decay before your equity does.
~1 day
Tier 3 · Self-learning
Adapt without gambling
LLM gate in shadow mode
The LlmFilterService exists but is unwired. Don't give it veto power — run it in shadow: log its verdict on every signal for 90 days, then measure whether vetoed trades actually underperformed. Promote to live veto only with statistical proof. (Most LLM gates fail this test; you want to know cheaply.)
~3 days + 90d data
Bayesian per-pair confidence sizing
Maintain a Beta(α,β) posterior on each pair's live win rate, updated per closed trade. Size = base risk × posterior confidence vs baseline. A pair drifting below its backtest WR automatically de-weights itself — graceful degradation, fully explainable, no black box.
~4 days
Contextual bandit over setup features
Each signal carries features (sweep depth in ATR, ADX, killzone, mode). A Thompson-sampling bandit learns which feature combinations earn full size vs half size. Bounded action space (sizing only, never entries), online learning, converges with hundreds — not millions — of samples.
~1–2 weeks
Deliberately excluded: full reinforcement-learning entry/exit policies. With ~30 live trades per month, an RL agent would need a decade of data to outlearn the validated rule set — and would silently overfit long before that. The architecture above keeps the proven edge as the backbone and lets learning adjust only confidence and size, where small samples are survivable. That's the smart version of self-learning: adapt the throttle, never the steering.
◆ Section · 06 Bottom line

The verdict

ARGUS is a validated, well-engineered, defensively excellent strategy — operating one tier below its potential.

The edge is real: three rounds of adversarial gate-testing failed to find a filter that improves it, which is the strongest evidence the signal is already distilled. The risk stack would survive scrutiny at a prop firm. What separates it from "highly profitable" is not the strategy — it's the feedback loop around it: a drift monitor currently showing garbage, no live-vs-backtest ledger, no correlation awareness, and adaptation mechanisms that don't exist yet.

Run Tier 1 this month. Tier 2 this quarter. Let Tier 3 earn its way in with shadow data. Expect live performance of +40–55%/yr at 1.5% risk if the backtest holds within a typical live haircut — and trust the DEGRADED panel to tell you early if it doesn't. Once it's fixed.
